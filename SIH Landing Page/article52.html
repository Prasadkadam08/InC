Article 52 : The Presidential Oath

In 1950, India was preparing to become a republic. Jawaharlal Nehru, the Prime Minister, was in a meeting with Dr. Rajendra Prasad, who was about to become the first President of India. Nehru: "Dr. Prasad, tomorrow you will take the oath as the first President of India. It’s a historic moment." Dr. Prasad smiled, knowing the weight of the responsibility. Dr. Prasad: "Yes, Jawaharlal. It’s an honor, but also a duty to uphold the Constitution and serve the people with integrity." Nehru: "Article 52 is simple, yet powerful. It states there shall be a President of India. But that role is much more than just a title. You will be the guardian of our democracy." Dr. Prasad nodded thoughtfully. Dr. Prasad: "I understand, and I am ready. I will be the symbol of unity for our people, representing the spirit of our nation." The next day, on January 26, 1950, in a grand ceremony, Dr. Rajendra Prasad took the oath of office as the first President of India. As he stood before the nation, he embodied the essence of Article 52—becoming a leader who would rise above politics, a symbol of the people's trust and the protector of the Constitution.
